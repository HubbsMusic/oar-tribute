O.A.R. - Of A Revolution

History of O.A.R.

  • Formation: O.A.R. was formed in 1996 in Rockville, Maryland.
  • Original Members: The band started with Marc Roberge (vocals, guitar), Chris Culos (drums), Richard On (guitar), and Benj Gershman (bass).
  • High School Origins: The founding members met and started playing music together in high school at Thomas S. Wootton High School in Rockville, MD.
  • College Years: They continued to play together when they attended Ohio State University.
  • Jerry DePizzo: Originally from Youngstown, Ohio, the sax-man joined O.A.R. shortly after the original members moved to Columbus to attend Ohio State University.
  • Debut Album: Their debut album, "The Wanderer," was released in 1997.
  • Early Sound: Their early music was a mix of rock and reggae, with a strong jam band feel.
  • Breakthrough Song: One of their first big hits was "That Was a Crazy Game of Poker" from their debut album.
  • Live Performances: O.A.R. is renowned for their energetic and interactive live performances.
  • Cult Following: The band gained a significant following through heavy touring and by word of mouth, without major media exposure or support from a record label.
  • First Major Label Album: They signed with Lava Records in 2003 and released their first major-label debut, "In Between Now and Then."
  • Hit Single: "Love and Memories," released in 2005, was a significant hit and received extensive radio play.
  • Live Album Success: Their live album, "Any Time Now," released in 2002, was a critical and commercial success.
  • Charity Work: The band is involved in various charitable efforts, particularly in support of youth and education initiatives. Their most notable project is the "Heard the World Foundation" ran by Marc's wife Nasim.

    • Video: Heard The World Foundation

  • Unique Sound: Their early music was a mix of rock and reggae, with a strong jam band feel.
  • Mikel Paris: A former member of the “Stomp” cast, the percussionist and keyboardist became an official member of the band in 2006, though he had played with them in various capacities prior to that.

    • Image: Mikel Paris

  • Album 'King': Their seventh studio album, "King," released in 2011, reached No. 12 on the Billboard 200 chart.
  • Jon Lampley: Joined O.A.R. in the 2010s, adding a new layer to the band’s sound with his brass instrumentals on trumpet and sousaphone.

    • Image: Jon Lampley

  • Musical Evolution: Over the years, their music has evolved, incorporating more pop and polished production elements.
  • Loyal Fanbase: They have a loyal fan base that make it rain with playing cards at every live show.
  • Record Sales: O.A.R. has sold millions of albums and concert tickets worldwide.
